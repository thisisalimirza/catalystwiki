You've been accepted. You've met your team. Now what? Use this checklist to get fully set up in your first week.

## Week 1 checklist

### Get connected
- [ ] Join the Catalyst Slack workspace (check your email for the invite)
- [ ] Introduce yourself in **#introductions** — name, specialty/background, and what problem space excites you
- [ ] Find your team's private channel and say hello
- [ ] Follow **#announcements** for program-wide updates
- [ ] Add your mentor's contact to your phone

### Get your tools
- [ ] Get your **Anthropic API key** from Arvind Rajan — email catalyst@mdplus.community with your team name
- [ ] Create a free account at [claude.ai](https://claude.ai) — you can start using Claude immediately without the API key
- [ ] Bookmark this wiki
- [ ] Set up a shared note-taking space with your team (Notion, Google Docs — your choice)

### Understand your project
- [ ] Read your sponsor's problem brief in full (for sponsored track), or write a one-paragraph description of your idea (for indie track)
- [ ] Schedule your first team meeting this week
- [ ] Book your first mentor meeting — ask your mentor to help you sanity-check the problem scope
- [ ] Read the [Problem Framing guide](/your-project/understanding-your-problem)

### Set expectations with your team
- [ ] Agree on a weekly meeting time
- [ ] Decide who's the "project lead" — the person responsible for keeping things moving
- [ ] Agree on how you'll make decisions when you disagree

<Callout type="tip" title="Don't skip the mentor meeting">
Your first mentor meeting is the most important one. Come with the problem statement and your biggest open question. Mentors can save you weeks of going in the wrong direction.
</Callout>

## What to work on in weeks 1–2

| Week | Focus |
|---|---|
| Week 1 | Team setup, understand the problem, first mentor meeting |
| Week 2 | User research — talk to 3–5 people who have this problem |
| Week 3 | Define what you'll actually build (your MVP scope) |
| Week 4+ | Start building |

Don't start building in week 1. Start by deeply understanding the problem.

## Common mistakes in week 1

**Jumping straight to building.** The #1 mistake. Spend the first two weeks understanding the problem and talking to users before touching any tools.

**Over-scoping.** Your MVP doesn't need to do everything. A tool that does one thing well and demonstrably helps a user is a 10x better demo than a half-built tool that tries to do ten things.

**Going silent.** The fellowship is designed for interaction. Show up to workshops, post in Slack, use your mentor. The fellows who build the best things are the most engaged ones.

**Worrying you're not technical enough.** You don't need to write code. Seriously. Read the [Build with AI](/build-with-ai/intro) and [No-code tools](/build-your-mvp/no-code-tools) sections — you'll be surprised what's possible.
