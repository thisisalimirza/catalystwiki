Answers to the questions we hear most often from fellows.

## About the program

**Do I need to know how to code?**
No. Many Catalyst fellows have zero coding experience and still build impressive MVPs. We provide guides to no-code tools, AI-powered builders, and direct technical support. That said, even a basic familiarity with tools like Claude can take you very far.

**How much time should I expect to spend per week?**
The program asks for roughly 10–15 hours/week. That includes your team's weekly meeting, mentor check-ins, workshops, and actual building time. It's a meaningful commitment — plan your summer schedule accordingly.

**Can I apply with a pre-formed team?**
Yes. Submit individual applications and note your teammates' names. You may use the same essay responses.

**I'm an international student/trainee — am I eligible?**
Contact catalyst@mdplus.community directly. Eligibility and stipend payments for international participants depend on visa status and will be assessed case-by-case.

**What if I get matched to a problem I don't like?**
Reach out to your program facilitator during the matching period — before the fellowship officially begins. Once teams are formed and kickoff has happened, re-matching is not possible, but we can help you find angles within the problem that excite you.

**Does the IP belong to me or the sponsor?**
This depends on your specific sponsorship agreement. Read your sponsor agreement carefully and ask your program facilitator if you have questions. For indie track projects, IP generally stays with the team.

## About building

**I have no technical background. What can I realistically build?**
A lot, actually. AI tools have dramatically lowered the floor for what non-technical people can build. Fellows with no coding background have built:
- Clinical decision-support chatbots using Claude
- Patient education apps on no-code platforms like Bubble
- Diagnostic aids using MedGemma for image analysis
- Survey and screening tools using Typeform + Zapier
- Structured clinical note generators with Claude

**What are the Anthropic credits for?**
Your team gets an allocation from the program's $30,000 Anthropic credit pool. This lets you use Claude's API to power features in your product — like a chatbot, a summarization tool, or a clinical note generator — without paying out of pocket. See the [Claude guide](/build-with-ai/claude-guide) for setup.

**What is MedGemma and do I have to use it?**
Google HAI-DEF (Health AI Developer Foundations) is one of our key sponsors. They provide a suite of free, open healthcare AI models — including MedGemma (clinical text and image understanding), CXR Foundation (chest X-rays), Path Foundation (pathology), Derm Foundation (dermatology), MedSigLIP (medical vision-language embeddings), and Health Acoustics (cough and breath analysis). You're not required to use them, but they open up powerful possibilities for healthcare use cases. See the [HAI-DEF guide](/build-with-ai/hai-def) and learn more at [goo.gle/hai-def](https://goo.gle/hai-def).

**Can we use other AI tools besides Claude and MedGemma?**
Yes. GPT-4, Gemini, open-source models — anything goes. Claude and MedGemma are the ones we've arranged free/discounted access for, but use whatever best fits your project.

**What counts as an MVP?**
A Minimum Viable Product is the simplest version of your idea that actually demonstrates value. It doesn't need to be production-ready or handle every edge case. It needs to show: (1) the problem is real, (2) your solution addresses it, (3) a real user could use it. See [Defining your MVP](/your-project/defining-your-mvp).

## About Demo Day

**What format is Demo Day?**
Each team gets a time slot to present: a short pitch (5–7 minutes) followed by a live demo and Q&A. Judges are a mix of sponsor representatives, investors, and clinical/technical experts.

**Do we need a working product for Demo Day?**
Yes — a live demo is expected. It doesn't need to be fully featured, but judges will expect to see the product actually work, not just a slide deck. Build something real.

**Who attends Demo Day?**
Sponsors, mentors, MDplus community members, invited investors, and members of the public who register. Plan for a mixed audience that includes both technical and non-technical attendees.
