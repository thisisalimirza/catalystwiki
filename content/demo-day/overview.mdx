Demo Day is the culmination of your 10 weeks of work. It's your moment to show the world what you built, why it matters, and what comes next. This page covers everything you need to know about the event itself.

## What Demo Day is

Demo Day is a live event where every Catalyst team presents their work to an audience of sponsors, mentors, investors, and MDplus community members. Think of it as a science fair crossed with a startup pitch competition.

**Format:** Each team gets approximately **12 minutes** total:
- 5–7 minutes: Pitch + live demo
- 5 minutes: Q&A from judges

**Judges:** A panel that includes sponsor representatives, healthcare investors, clinicians, and technical experts. Composition varies; it will be announced before Demo Day.

**Audience:** Beyond the judge panel, the event is open to registered attendees — expect a mix of technical and non-technical healthcare professionals, fellow MDplus community members, and guests of the sponsors.

## What judges are looking for

Judges evaluate teams on four dimensions:

### 1. Problem clarity
Do you clearly articulate who has this problem, how severe it is, and why it hasn't been solved? Judges see hundreds of pitches. Vague problem statements are immediately dismissive. Specific, well-evidenced problems are compelling.

### 2. Solution quality
Does your solution actually address the problem? Is the MVP working and demonstrable? Is the approach sensible given real healthcare constraints (workflow, regulation, adoption)? Judges are not just evaluating the idea — they're evaluating whether the team has thought clearly about what it takes to make this work in practice.

### 3. User evidence
Have you talked to real users? What did you learn? Did their feedback change what you built? A team that says "we talked to 12 nurses and changed our design based on X" is far more credible than one that says "we think clinicians will want this."

### 4. Viability and next steps
What would it take to make this real? What's the business model? Who pays for it? What are the regulatory considerations? Judges don't expect you to have all the answers — but they expect you to have thought about the questions.

## What a great Demo Day performance looks like

- **You know your problem cold.** When a judge asks "how many patients does this affect?" or "what does the current workflow look like?", you have an immediate, specific answer.
- **Your live demo works.** The product loads, the AI responds, the core flow runs end-to-end without a crash.
- **Your pitch is rehearsed but not robotic.** You've practiced enough that you're not reading slides, but you can still respond naturally to the room.
- **You handle hard questions gracefully.** When a judge asks something you don't know, you say "That's a great question — we haven't figured that out yet, and here's how we'd approach it."
- **You're excited about it.** Energy is contagious. If you're genuinely excited about what you built and the problem you're solving, that comes through and it matters.

## Prizes and recognition

Prize structure will be announced at the Week 9 pitch prep workshop. Historically, prizes have included:

- Grand prize (cash + continued engagement)
- Runner-up prizes
- Category awards (Best Clinical Design, Best Technical Implementation, Best User Evidence, etc.)
- Sponsor-specific awards from individual sponsor companies

Beyond formal prizes: Demo Day is a genuine networking event. Sponsors may approach teams about continuing to work together. Investors may follow up. The MDplus network is watching.

## Logistics

**Date:** Mid-August 2026 (exact date confirmed at kickoff)

**Format:** In-person preferred; hybrid option for remote teams confirmed

**Duration:** Full-day event including all team presentations, networking lunch, Q&A, and prize announcements

**What to bring:**
- Your laptop (charged, with charger)
- A printed one-pager about your project (optional but useful for networking)
- Your Figma mockups or architecture diagrams if useful during Q&A
- Business cards or a QR code to your LinkedIn (for networking after)

**Tech setup:** Each team gets a presentation slot with access to a shared projector/screen. Bring your own HDMI adapter. Test your presentation on the shared screen during the setup window before the event starts.
