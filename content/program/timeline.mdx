A week-by-week guide to what happens across the 10-week fellowship.

## Pre-fellowship

| Date | Milestone |
|---|---|
| Early May 2026 | Applications open |
| May 17, 2026 | Application deadline |
| Late May 2026 | Team formation & sponsor matching |
| Early June 2026 | Fellowship agreements signed; API keys issued |

## The 10 weeks

### Week 1 — Kickoff
**Theme: Know your problem and your team**

- Kickoff event (all fellows, sponsors, mentors)
- Team introductions in Slack
- First mentor meeting
- Read your sponsor brief (or draft your indie problem statement)
- **Submit:** Team intro + problem statement

### Week 2 — User research
**Theme: Talk to people before you build anything**

- Conduct 3–5 user interviews (patients, clinicians, admins — whoever your target user is)
- Workshop: "How to run a user interview"
- Identify the top 2–3 pain points your users mention

### Week 3 — Synthesis + scoping
**Theme: Decide what you're building**

- Synthesize your research into a clear problem statement
- Define your MVP scope — the minimum version of your solution
- Workshop: "What is an MVP and how do you scope one?"
- **Submit:** User research summary + MVP scope doc

<Callout type="warning" title="The most important decision you'll make">
Your MVP scope is the most consequential decision of the fellowship. Scope too big and you'll run out of time. Scope too small and your demo won't be compelling. Use your mentor and the workshop to pressure-test your scope before you start building.
</Callout>

### Week 4 — Build sprint begins
**Theme: Start building**

- Set up your tools (Bubble, Claude, whatever you've chosen)
- Get your first working prototype — even if rough and manual
- Workshop: "Intro to no-code tools" or "Using Claude in your product"

### Week 5 — First working prototype
**Theme: Something exists**

- You should have something a user could click through by end of this week
- It doesn't need to work perfectly — it needs to exist
- Get feedback from at least one user on the prototype
- Workshop: "Design basics for non-designers"

### Week 6 — Mid-program check-in
**Theme: Course correction**

- 5-minute demo/progress update to the full cohort
- Feedback from facilitators and other fellows
- Adjust your scope based on what you've learned
- **Submit:** Mid-program check-in demo

### Week 7 — Iteration
**Theme: Make it better**

- Incorporate feedback from the check-in
- Focus on the core user journey — make it smooth end-to-end
- Workshop: "Healthcare data & privacy basics"

### Week 8 — Polish sprint
**Theme: Make it real**

- Get your core flow to a state you'd feel comfortable demoing live
- Start thinking about your Demo Day narrative
- Workshop: "Pitching for non-founders"

### Week 9 — Demo prep
**Theme: Tell the story**

- Practice your Demo Day pitch with your mentor
- Record a backup screen recording in case live tech fails
- Finalize your slide deck (5–7 slides max)
- **Submit:** Final deck draft for feedback

### Week 10 — Demo Day
**Theme: Show the world**

- Demo Day event — live pitches, Q&A, networking
- Celebrate — you built something real in 10 weeks
- **Submit:** Final milestone check-in

## After Demo Day

- Completion stipends processed (sponsored track)
- Prize announcements
- Sponsor follow-up conversations (for teams sponsors want to continue working with)
- MDplus alumni network access
