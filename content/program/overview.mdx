The **Catalyst Fellowship** is a 10-week summer program that matches healthcare professionals with real, sponsor-defined healthcare problems — or supports fellows who bring their own — to build technology solutions from the ground up.

<Callout type="info" title="Quick facts">
- **Duration:** 10 weeks, Summer 2026
- **Kickoff:** Week of June 8, 2026
- **Demo Day:** Mid-August 2026
- **Time commitment:** 10–15 hours/week
- **Contact:** catalyst@mdplus.community
</Callout>

## The two tracks

### Sponsored track
You're matched with a company sponsor who has submitted a defined problem brief. The company funds a $1,000 completion stipend and provides domain access — subject matter experts you can interview, data you can reference (de-identified), and a real organizational context for your solution.

**Who this is for:** Fellows who want clear direction, an engaged stakeholder, and a defined problem to solve.

### Indie track
You bring your own problem — an idea from your clinical work, research, or personal experience. You have full creative control, access to all program mentorship and workshops, and eligibility for grand prizes. No guaranteed stipend.

**Who this is for:** Fellows with a strong prior idea and the self-direction to define their own problem and scope.

<Callout type="tip" title="Not sure which is right for you?">
If you have a burning idea you've been sitting on, indie track. If you're excited about the problem space but want more structure, sponsored track. Both tracks get the same mentorship, AI credits, and workshop access.
</Callout>

## What sponsors provide

Sponsors are companies and health systems that have identified real problems they want solved. They commit to:

- A written problem brief (submitted before kickoff)
- At minimum one dedicated company contact who meets with your team monthly
- Domain access: interviews, data references, and context
- The $1,000 completion stipend for fellows on their project

What sponsors are **not** providing: a finished product spec, a tech team to build for you, or a guarantee of adoption. Your job is to take the problem brief and turn it into something real.

## Mentors

Mentors are experienced professionals — clinicians, founders, engineers, designers, investors — who volunteer their expertise. Each team is matched with at least one mentor for the fellowship duration.

Mentors commit to approximately **2–4 hours/month** — typically a monthly 1-hour video call plus occasional async questions.

**How to get the most out of your mentor:** Come to every meeting with a specific question or decision you need help making. Don't use mentor time for status updates — use it for advice, connections, and perspective you can't get elsewhere. See the [Working with mentors guide](/resources/working-with-mentors).

## Weekly programming

The fellowship includes structured programming every week:

- **Workshops** — Technical and domain sessions run by sponsors, mentors, and external experts (AI tools, product thinking, regulatory basics, pitching)
- **Check-ins** — Brief async updates submitted to your facilitator every week (what did you do, what's blocking you)
- **Office hours** — Drop-in sessions for technical and product questions
- **Demo checkpoints** — Mid-program check-in presentations to the cohort (no grades, just feedback)

## What you'll submit and when

| Milestone | When | What |
|---|---|---|
| Team intro + problem statement | Week 1 | 1-paragraph description of your project |
| User research summary | Week 3 | 3–5 user interviews summarized |
| MVP scope doc | Week 4 | What you're building and why |
| Mid-program check-in | Week 6 | 5-min demo/progress update to cohort |
| Demo Day | Week 10 | Full pitch + live demo |

None of these are graded. They're checkpoints to keep you on track and get feedback.

## Completion requirements

To complete the fellowship (and receive a stipend, if applicable):

1. Participate in at least 6 of 10 weekly workshops
2. Submit all milestone check-ins (late is okay; missing is not)
3. Present at Demo Day

That's it. The quality of your work will vary — what matters is that you ship something real and show up.
