Catalyst's workshop programming is one of the most valuable parts of the fellowship. This page describes what to expect and how to get the most out of each session.

<Callout type="info" title="Attendance policy">
Attend at least 6 of 10 weekly workshops to satisfy the completion requirement. All workshops are recorded and posted in Slack within 24 hours for async access.
</Callout>

## How workshops work

Most workshops run **60–75 minutes** via video call. Format varies:
- **Lecture + Q&A** — Expert presents, then takes questions (weeks 1, 4, 7)
- **Workshop/hands-on** — You follow along and build something during the session (weeks 5 for both AI sessions)
- **Peer feedback** — You present to the cohort and get structured feedback (weeks 6, 9)

## Key workshops to prioritize

### Week 5: Using Claude in your Product
This is the workshop that most transforms non-technical fellows. You'll learn, hands-on, how to:
- Set up your Anthropic API key
- Send your first API call using no-code tools
- Build a working AI feature into your product within the session

Come with your laptop and a rough sense of what AI feature you want to build.

### Week 5: Google MedGemma for Healthcare AI
The Google HAI-DEF team will walk through what MedGemma can and can't do, demo it live on clinical tasks, and explain how your team can access it for free as a Catalyst fellow. If your project involves anything clinical — notes, imaging, diagnosis — this session is critical.

### Week 7: Healthcare Data, Privacy & HIPAA Basics
Many fellows underestimate this. Your MVP probably doesn't need to be HIPAA-compliant during the fellowship — but you need to understand what you can and can't do with patient data in your demo, and what a real deployment would require. This workshop will give you a practical map.

### Week 9: Pitching for Non-Founders
The way you tell your story matters as much as what you built. This session will cover the narrative structure that works for Demo Day, how to handle technical questions from non-technical judges (and vice versa), and how to make your live demo not terrifying.

## Getting the most out of workshops

- **Come prepared.** Each workshop has a short prep task posted in Slack the day before. Do it — 15 minutes of prep makes the session 3x more valuable.
- **Ask questions.** The speaker list for these workshops is genuinely impressive. Use the time.
- **Bring your specific problem.** The best workshop moments are when a fellow asks "our project is doing X — does that change your advice?" Be that fellow.
- **Watch recordings if you miss one.** Recordings are in the **#workshop-recordings** Slack channel within 24 hours.
