The Catalyst cohort is a community, not just a program. The connections you make with your fellow fellows, mentors, and sponsors are often as valuable as anything you build. This page covers how to engage.

## Slack workspace

Slack is the primary communication hub for the fellowship. Your invitation will come from catalyst@mdplus.community at the start of the program.

### Key channels

| Channel | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **#announcements** | Program-wide announcements — read every post |
| **#introductions** | Introduce yourself at the start of the program |
| **#general** | General fellowship chatter, wins, questions |
| **#technical-help** | Get unblocked on product/tech questions |
| **#ai-help** | Claude API, MedGemma, and AI-specific questions |
| **#google-hidef** | Direct line to the Google HiDef team |
| **#mentor-office-hours** | Booking links and async mentor Q&A |
| **#workshop-recordings** | Recordings of all weekly workshops |
| **#demo-day-prep** | Peer feedback and demo prep resources |
| **#requests** | Request tools, introductions, or resources |
| **#random** | Everything else — share what you're working on |

Your team will also have a private channel created for you.

### Slack etiquette

- **Use threads.** Reply in threads on messages to keep channels readable.
- **Be specific when asking for help.** "I'm stuck" gets slower help than "I'm trying to call the Claude API from Bubble and getting a 401 error — here's my API Connector setup [screenshot]."
- **Share your wins.** When something works, post it in #general. The cohort energy rises when people celebrate each other.
- **Don't be afraid to ask "dumb" questions.** There are no dumb questions in #technical-help. Everyone started somewhere.

## The peer cohort

Your fellow fellows are one of your best resources. They're dealing with the same challenges, have complementary skills, and will become part of your professional network long after the program ends.

**Peer collaboration is encouraged.** You can:
- Ask other teams for feedback on your pitch or product
- Share tools or prompts that worked for you
- Help each other debug technical issues
- Review each other's user research findings

**What stays within teams:** Your specific product design, sponsor relationships, and proprietary problem briefs belong to your team.

## Weekly check-in format

Each week, submit a brief async check-in to your facilitator by Sunday evening. Format:

```
Team: [Team name]
Week: [#]

What we accomplished this week:
- [Item]
- [Item]

What's blocking us:
- [Blocker or "nothing"]

What we're doing next week:
- [Item]
- [Item]

Support needed:
- [Request or "nothing"]
```

These take 5 minutes to write and keep the program team in the loop so they can proactively unblock you.

## Getting unblocked quickly

When you're stuck, the fastest path to getting unblocked:

1. **Check this wiki first.** The most common questions are answered here.
2. **Post in #technical-help or #ai-help.** Include context: what you're trying to do, what you've tried, what happened. Mentors and fellow fellows monitor these channels.
3. **DM your facilitator** for program-specific issues (mentor conflict, milestone question, event logistics).
4. **Email catalyst@mdplus.community** for anything that needs a formal response.

Expected response times: Slack channels within 24 hours during the week. Email within 48 hours.

## MDplus community beyond Catalyst

Catalyst is part of the broader MDplus community — a network of physician-innovators, healthcare entrepreneurs, and clinical leaders.

After the fellowship, you'll have access to:
- MDplus alumni Slack
- Community events and speaker series
- Warm introductions within the network (pay it forward)

The MDplus community is how many former fellows found co-founders, clinical advisors, job opportunities, and investors. Engage authentically and stay connected.

## Mental health and wellbeing

Building a product while managing clinical training or practice is genuinely hard. A few reminders:

- **It's okay to not know things.** Everyone in this fellowship is figuring things out in real time. Imposter syndrome is normal. Act anyway.
- **Progress over perfection.** A working rough product beats a perfect unfinished one. Ship ugly. Iterate.
- **Reach out when you're struggling.** If you're feeling overwhelmed, stuck, or like you're falling behind, tell your facilitator early — not at the deadline. We can help.
- **Protect your clinical commitments.** The fellowship is designed to fit around your clinical schedule. If you have a conflict (boards, clinical blocks, call schedule), let your facilitator know and we'll accommodate where we can.

You signed up for this because you care about improving healthcare. That motivation is the most important thing. Protect it.
